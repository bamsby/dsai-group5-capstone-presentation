Executive Summary
What this POC is — and is not
🏗
The Problem
CP report preparation is manual, fragmented, and time-intensive. Inspection knowledge is scattered across drones, close-range reports, images, and prior documents.
🤖
The Solution
An AWS Agentic RAG assistant that ingests inspection data, retrieves relevant evidence, and drafts structured, regulation-aligned report sections for CP review.
🔒
The Approach
Two-agent architecture with governance guardrails, human-in-the-loop checkpoints, and IMDA Model Governance Framework alignment.
✅
The Outcome
POC validates technical feasibility, output quality, and operational cost — informing the decision to scale to production.
This system augments professional judgment. All outputs are draft assistance requiring CP validation and sign-off.

What is PFI and Why It Matters
2022
Year PFI regime commenced
(effective 1 Jan 2022)
7 yrs
Mandatory inspection cycle
per building
20 yr / 13m
Building age and height
threshold for compliance
Mandatory under Singapore BCA Building Control Act
Applies to buildings >20 years old and >13m in height (with statutory exclusions)
Competent Person (CP) must be appointed for each inspection cycle
Scope: 100% visual inspection + ≥10% close-range inspection per building face
CP submits formal report; repair works carried out as recommended by CP
Non-compliance may result in enforcement action by the Commissioner of Building Control.

09 / 15
Scope Control
Scope Control by Design — Not as an Afterthought
What the System Will Not Do
STRUCTURAL SAFETY
No structural engineering advice beyond inspection scope
LEGAL & LIABILITY
No legal conclusions or liability assignments
FINANCIAL ESTIMATES
No repair cost estimation
SAFETY GUARANTEES
No safety certifications or absolute assurances
INAPPROPRIATE CONTENT
True threats, defamation, obscenity, fighting words blocked
Why These Guardrails Exist
Prevent out-of-scope professional advice that requires separate expertise
Protect users from relying on AI for legal or structural decisions
Ensure outputs remain within the role of a reporting assistant
Comply with responsible AI standards and BCA professional standards
Reinforce that CP professional judgment and accountability is irreplaceable
"The system is a draft assistant. It does not replace the Competent Person."

Governance
Aligned with Singapore's IMDA Model Governance Framework for Agentic AI

Risk Assessment & Scoping
Define agent boundaries and limit tool permissions
Standardized risk assessment across harm severity, autonomy, data sensitivity
IAM controls, audit logging, ADLC governance
PILLAR 2
Governance & Human Accountability
RACI model with clear ownership per system component
Human-in-the-loop at report review, output flagging, and deployment gates
Oversight from design through deployment and monitoring
PILLAR 3
Technical Controls & Reliability
Advanced RAG: recursive retrieval, dynamic weighting, multi-hop verification
Input + output guardrails with semantic constraints
Continuous evaluation of retrieval quality and generation faithfulness
PILLAR 4
User Empowerment & Training
Mandatory AI literacy (AI 101) for all users
UI disclaimers and human verification reminders on every output
Transparency of system capabilities, limitations, and denied topics
Reference: IMDA Model Governance Framework for Agentic AI (2024)
